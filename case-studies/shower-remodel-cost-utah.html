Shower Remodel Cost in Utah

Most homeowners want one number — but a durable Utah shower is built from waterproofing, tile scope, fixtures, and careful sequencing. Here is how those pieces tend to line up on estimates we prepare in Salt Lake County.

Scope

What moves the number first

Wall footprint, ceiling height, number of plumbing fixture changes, and whether we replace pan/curb or convert layout all shift labor and materials. In Utah, we also plan grout and seal strategies for mineral-heavy water so finishes hold up.

Typical line items homeowners see

  • Demolition, haul-off, and jobsite protection
  • Substrate repair if sheet goods fail inspection
  • Waterproofing system, pan, and drainage work
  • Tile materials plus niche, bench, or accent detail
  • Fixture package — valve, trim, door or enclosure

Ranges vary widely; we provide written ranges tied to your selections rather than a generic square-foot guess.

Budget clarity

Where upgrades earn their keep

Epoxy grout in key areas, properly installed waterproofing, and a drain system suited to the new flow matter more than chasing the cheapest tile. Those choices reduce callbacks and maintenance — especially along the Wasatch Front.

During estimates, we flag decisions that swing price the most: enclosure type, tile format and pattern complexity, moving plumbing, and specialty glass.

Estimates

How we structure pricing

You receive an itemized proposal showing labor buckets and allowances for fixtures and tile. If selections change, we update the scope before ordering materials so pricing stays aligned with the finished shower you approved.

If you are comparing contractors, ask whether waterproofing, permits, and haul-off are included — apples-to-apples comparisons only work when scope matches.
